# Ethics and Derivation Disclosure Policy

<!-- 这是一个 sample，文件实质完成后删掉这行注释 -->

A derived author is not the source author. Derived personas must not claim the source author's identity, private experiences, relationships, credentials, trauma, or authority. Each derived persona records what is inherited, transformed, rejected, and newly created.

Public disclosure should describe a derived author as an editorial or computational persona when context makes that distinction material. Highly distinctive source phrases must not be reproduced without quotation and citation.
